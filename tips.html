LITTLE BITS YOU SHOULD KNOW!

  • Limit and monitor the use of third-party devices: Be vigilant about connecting third-party devices, such as USB drives or smartphones, to your organization's computers or network. Only use trusted, authorized devices and ensure they have updated antivirus software installed.

    Scan any external devices for viruses or malware before connecting them to your workstation or the organization's systems. Additionally, follow your organization's policies regarding the use of personal or third-party devices to minimize the risk of introducing viruses or malware into the care home's network.

  • Implement and follow strict data sharing protocols: As a healthcare worker, you should adhere to your organization's data sharing policies and procedures to prevent the dissemination of sensitive information to unauthorized individuals. Always verify the identity and authorization of the recipient before sharing any confidential data.

    Use secure and encrypted communication channels when transmitting sensitive information, and avoid discussing confidential details in public spaces or overheard conversations. By diligently following data sharing protocols and maintaining a high level of awareness, you can help protect sensitive data and prevent unauthorized access.

  • Consistently adhere to established procedures and data security policies: As a healthcare worker, it's crucial to consistently follow your organization's established procedures and data security policies. Familiarize yourself with these guidelines and ensure that you and your colleagues adhere to them at all times.

    Regularly attend training sessions and stay updated on any changes or updates to these policies. Encourage open communication and a culture of accountability within your team, promoting a shared responsibility for upholding security protocols. By prioritizing adherence to established procedures and data security policies, you contribute to maintaining a strong security posture and reducing the risk of breaches in your care home.

  • Be cautious with links in emails and social media: As a healthcare worker, always verify the destination URL by hovering over embedded links before clicking. Be skeptical of unexpected or unsolicited emails, and avoid clicking on links requesting sensitive information. Use updated antivirus software, and when in doubt, confirm the legitimacy of a link with the sender through a separate channel.

  • Be cautious with files and images from external sources: As a healthcare worker, only download files and images from reputable sources, and scan them with antivirus software before use. Be wary of unexpected files from unknown sources, adhere to organizational guidelines, and promptly report any suspicious activities or potential compromises.
