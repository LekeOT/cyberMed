WHAT YOU MUST KNOW ABOUT DDoS ATTACKS

What is Distributed Denial of Service attack?

A DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) attack is a cyberattack where multiple systems, usually compromised by malware, work together to flood a targeted website or network with an overwhelming amount of traffic.

This bombardment of requests causes the target's resources to become overloaded, making the website or network slow or entirely unavailable for legitimate users.

How can you detect DDoS attacks in your workplace?

These are some important tips on how to detect DDoS attacks in your workplace as healthcare workers:

  • Slow network performance: If websites, applications, or internal systems suddenly become very slow or unresponsive, it could be an indication of a DDoS attack.

  • Inability to access online resources: If multiple users are unable to access specific websites or network resources, it could be a sign of a targeted attack.

  • Intermittent connectivity: Frequent disconnects and reconnects or fluctuating network performance could be a symptom of an ongoing DDoS attack.

  • Unexplained spikes in network traffic: If your organization monitors network traffic, unusual spikes or patterns could signal a DDoS attack.

  • Educate yourself and colleagues: Stay informed about the latest DDoS threats and their indicators, and share this knowledge with your colleagues to create a more secure workplace.

Prevention is better than cure

These are simple and straightforward ways you can prevent DDoS attacks:

  • Report unusual network activity: Promptly inform your IT department of any suspicious network behavior or performance issues, as this information can help them detect and mitigate potential DDoS attacks.

  • Participate in cybersecurity training: SRegularly attend cybersecurity training sessions to stay informed about potential threats, including DDoS attacks, and learn the best practices for maintaining a secure network environment.

  • Practice good cybersecurity hygiene: Use strong, unique passwords, enable multi-factor authentication, and keep your devices updated with the latest security patches to reduce the risk of your devices being compromised and used in a DDoS attack.
