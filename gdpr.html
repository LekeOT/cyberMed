WHAT YOU MUST KNOW ABOUT THE GDPR

What is The GDPR?

The GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) is a comprehensive data privacy regulation that came into effect in the European Union on May 25, 2018. It aims to protect the personal data of EU citizens by giving them more control over their information and ensuring that businesses handle data in a transparent and secure manner. The regulation applies to all organizations, regardless of their location, that process the personal data of individuals within the EU.

How you can abide by the GDPR rules...

These are simple and straightforward ways you can abide by the GDPR set rules:

  • Adhere to data protection policies: Familiarize yourself with your organization's data protection policies and procedures, and strictly adhere to them when handling personal data.

  • Participate in training: Regularly attend cybersecurity and data privacy training sessions to stay updated on GDPR requirements and best practices for maintaining a secure environment.

  • Practice good cybersecurity hygiene: Use strong, unique passwords, enable multi-factor authentication, and keep your devices updated with the latest security patches to reduce the risk of data breaches.

  • Limit data access: Only access the personal data of patients or residents that is necessary for your job role and avoid sharing sensitive information with unauthorized individuals.

  • Report incidents: Promptly report any suspected data breaches, security incidents, or potential GDPR violations to your organization's Data Protection Officer (DPO) or appropriate authority.

  • Secure data storage and disposal: Store personal data securely, using encryption where necessary, and ensure the proper disposal of physical or electronic records containing sensitive information.

  • Maintain confidentiality: Respect the privacy of patients and residents by discussing personal data only in appropriate settings and not disclosing confidential information to unauthorized individuals.

  • Obtain consent: Ensure that you obtain explicit consent from patients or residents before collecting, processing, or sharing their personal data, in accordance with GDPR guidelines.

  • Support data subject rights: Assist individuals in exercising their GDPR rights, such as the right to access, correct, or delete their personal data, by following your organization's procedures.
